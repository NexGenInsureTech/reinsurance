The Key Protagonists

Three key players dominate the reinsurance landscape, each with their own unique motivations, goals, and frustrations with the current system.

The Ceding Insurer

The buyer of protection. Seeks to manage capital, stabilize earnings, and protect against catastrophes.

Wants: Best price, broad coverage, fast placement.

Pains: Opaque pricing, slow process, limited market access.

üìà

The Reinsurer

The seller of protection. Aims for underwriting profit by building a diversified portfolio of risks.

Wants: High-quality data, adequate premium, disciplined partners.

Pains: Poor data, flawed analytics, outdated portfolio views.

The Broker

The critical intermediary. Advises the cedent and navigates the market to place the risk.

Wants: Client satisfaction, efficient placement, market influence.

Pains: Manual admin work, communication chaos, process bottlenecks.

The Path Forward

Success requires a deliberate, phased approach to manage complexity, build trust, and gain market momentum. A "big bang" launch is doomed to fail.

1

Phase 1: The Wedge

Launch a POC/MVP focused on the more transactional facultative market in a single geography. Target brokers as the primary users to prove value and build initial liquidity.

2

Phase 2: Expansion

Expand into adjacent business lines and geographies. Add more sophisticated analytics and begin marketing directly to ceding companies. Leverage the network effect to accelerate growth.

3

Phase 3: The Holy Grail

With a mature platform and trusted brand, begin tackling the highly complex and relationship-driven treaty market. Evolve into the core infrastructure for the entire industry.

Pain Point: Submission Prep

  • Unstructured Data: Risk data is buried in PDFs, Word docs, and messy spreadsheets.
  • Manual Entry: High risk of human error, typos, and missing information from the start.
  • No Standards: Every submission is different, making it impossible for reinsurers to automate data ingestion.
  • Time Consuming: Broker teams spend countless hours manually compiling and cleaning data.

Pain Point: Broker Marketing

  • Email Chaos: The entire process runs on massive email chains with dozens of recipients and attachments.
  • Manual Tracking: Brokers use spreadsheets to manually track quotes, questions, and declinations. It's slow and error-prone.
  • Limited Reach: Market access is often limited by the broker's personal network, potentially missing better capacity.
  • No Transparency: The cedent has little to no real-time visibility into the placement's progress.

Pain Point: Underwriting & Analysis

  • Data Re-keying: Underwriters manually type data from the submission into their own pricing models, wasting time and risking errors.
  • Analytic Bottlenecks: Poor data quality cripples sophisticated pricing tools, forcing reliance on slow, manual analysis.
  • Outdated Portfolio Views: Underwriters make decisions based on portfolio data that can be weeks old, leading to suboptimal risk selection.
  • Repetitive Questions: Multiple reinsurers ask the same clarifying questions independently, creating communication overhead.

Pain Point: Finalizing the Deal

  • Manual Documentation: Reinsurance slips and full contract wordings are created manually, a meticulous but slow process.
  • Version Control Issues: Managing changes and ensuring all parties agree on the final terms can be difficult.
  • Compliance Burden: Ensuring every contract meets complex, multi-jurisdictional regulations is a significant challenge.
  • Inefficient Payments: Premium and claims settlements often rely on antiquated processes, increasing back-office costs.
